How to read this — please read first.
  • "Positive reply rate" = of the follow-ups with a given trait, the share whose next reply was interested or booked.
  • We compare that with vs. without the trait. "{{ "%.1f"|format(2.0) }}× the reply rate" means roughly double the replies — but this is a correlation, not proof that the wording caused it.
  • Biggest caveat: {{ overview.warm_rate }}% of these follow-ups went to leads who were already interested before the email was sent — so high numbers often reflect a warm lead, not great writing.

Does it pay to keep following up?

Earlier follow-ups show higher reply rates — but mostly because once a lead replies positively, we stop following up. So this is survival bias, not proof that early follow-ups are better written. Read it as "most positive replies come early," not "stop after #3."
