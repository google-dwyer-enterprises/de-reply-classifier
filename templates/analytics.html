How to read this page — a 30-second guide
  • "Reply rate" means: out of the emails written a certain way, how many got a reply that was interested or booked a call.
  • We compare the reply rate when a style was used against when it wasn't. These are patterns we spotted, not proof — a style that got more replies didn't necessarily cause them.
  • Most important: {{ overview.warm_rate }}% of these emails went to people who had already shown interest before the email was sent. So a high score often means the person was already keen — not that the writing won them over.
  • One client sent nearly half of all these emails, so anything marked Mostly one client may just be that one client's habit. Not enough data means too few emails to trust the number.

Does it pay to keep following up?

The 1st and 2nd follow-ups show higher reply rates — but that's mostly because once someone replies, we stop emailing them. So it really just means most replies come early, not that later follow-ups are written worse. Don't read it as "stop after the 3rd."
