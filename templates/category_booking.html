How to read this — and what it can't tell you
  • Descriptive, not proof. It shows where bookings happened, not that a category causes them. Check the "share of bookings" before chasing a high rate on a small base.
  • Coverage: {{ booked_cat }} of {{ booked_total }} bookings ({{ cov_pct }}%) have a category; {{ via_domain }} were recovered via their company domain. The remaining {{ uncat }} (mostly leads whose reply address isn't in the enrichment pool) are excluded from the ranking.
  • Confidence: groups under {{ min_engaged }} engaged aren't ranked; every ranked rate carries a Wilson 95% interval — wide ranges mean "not enough data to trust the order yet."
  • Domain-borrow assumption: leads on the same company domain are treated as the same category — safe for single-brand companies, looser for conglomerates.
  • Category labels come from enrichment and mix Amazon browse categories (which dominate the ranked list) with a long tail of other industry labels; only categories with real volume are ranked.
