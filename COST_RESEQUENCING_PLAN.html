2Two myths the data killed

BeliefWhat the measurements say
"Deeper search pages get more expensive"Refuted. Healthy industry segments were flat (3.3–5.1 credits/accepted) across all page depths. The one expensive run (11.6) came from exhausted segments scraped without the US/CA filter — so the fix is segment health tracking, not page resets.
"Leads without an email cost nothing"Doubtful. BC's own API example implies a small fee per returned result slot, and our books show 1.35 credits per stored lead. A 2-minute probe (§5) settles it.

3What the providers support that we don't use (verified in their docs)

UNUSED — FREE WIN

BetterContact: server-side title & seniority filters

The Lead Finder accepts lead_job_title include/exclude and a lead_seniority enum (owner, founder, c_suite, vp, head, director…) — exactly our decision-maker rule. Today we pay first and filter titles afterwards.
UNUSED — FREE WIN

BetterContact: company exclusion lists

company.exclude ("domains that must NOT match") lets every search exclude companies we've already maxed out (3 contacts) and every company we've ever rejected — resellers, MLMs, services, corporates. This attacks the single biggest waste class (duplicates, 22–57% of paid rejects) before billing. Contact-level exclusion via LinkedIn URLs also exists.
STRUCTURAL OPTION

Prospeo: search now, pay for emails later (meeting task #5 — feasible)

Prospeo's 2026 API is explicitly two-stage: Search Person returns people (name, title, company) without emails at 1 credit per 25 people; Enrich then charges 1 credit only per email actually found, only for the people we choose. That means our entire QA stack — dedup, titles, categories, the full website-verification funnel (it only needs the domain) — can run before a single email is paid for. BetterContact has no such mode (confirmed), but its separate enrichment API can backstop emails Prospeo misses.
COST TRAP AVOIDED

Phones cost 10× (meeting task #1)

Phone enrichment is 10 credits per number on both providers. Enabling it at scrape time would multiply waste by ~11× on every discarded lead. Correct placement: enrich phones only for approved leads (like the MillionVerifier gate) — or only for leads that reply.

4The resequenced pipeline

One principle, applied to money: every lead is rejected by the cheapest stage capable of rejecting it — ideally before it's paid for.

Track 1 — stop buying waste BC credits · the big money

  • Server-side titles/seniority — mirror our decision-maker tiers into the search itself; local check stays as backstop.
  • Suppression lists on every search — exclude capped companies + all previously-rejected company domains.
  • Segment health tracking — per-industry credits/accepted and duplicate-share; auto-park segments that go stale, US/CA filter stays default.

Track 2 — QA before payment structural end-state

  • Prospeo two-stage pilot (~$10–20): search 500 people → run the full existing QA on their companies (free — needs only domains) → buy emails only for survivors → standard review/verification. Measures coverage, email match-rate, and final quality vs BetterContact before any switch.
  • Phones post-approval only.

Track 3 — AI/token hygiene ~$40–90/mo + faster batches

  • Free contact-cap check moves before the AI gate (today: 5 contacts judged, 3 kept).
  • AI gate becomes per-company + cached + parallel — measured 42% duplicate judgments today.
  • Web-search consolidation: skip the ownership search for Amazon-registry-confirmed brands with zero risk signals; worst case drops from 4 searches/company.

6Expected outcome (at 20,000 leads/month)

Shown in credits so the percentages hold regardless of plan pricing (action item: confirm the actual $/credit on both provider plans):

Today — 4.16 credits per accepted lead (~84k credits/mo)
After Track 1 — ~2.5–3.0 cr/accepted (−30–40%)
After Track 2 (if pilot passes) — ~1.3–1.7 cr/accepted (−60–70%)
Honest bounds. Track 1's floor is the junk no server-side filter can express (~10–15% of paid leads will always be judged post-payment). Track 2's numbers live or die on Prospeo's person coverage and email match-rate for our exact ICP — that is precisely what the $10–20 pilot measures, and BetterContact remains the unchanged fallback if it fails.
What does not change: lead quality. Every QA layer stays; the review queue stays; the no-false-rejection principle stays. This plan only changes when checks run relative to when money is spent — the leads that reach Victor and Jam are the same or better, at a fraction of the cost.
