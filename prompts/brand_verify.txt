You are verifying whether a company is a PRODUCT BRAND or a RESELLER, using
evidence extracted from its actual website. This runs after an ICP filter, so
assume the company sells physical products — the only question is whose.

DEFINITIONS
- brand: the company sells products under ITS OWN brand identity. It may
  manufacture them or private-label them (private-label counts as a brand).
  One consistent brand identity across the site.
- reseller: the company's catalog is primarily OTHER companies' brands —
  a retailer, dealer, distributor, or multi-brand shop. The products' brand
  names are not the company's own.
- unknown: the evidence is insufficient or genuinely conflicting.

RESELLER SIGNALS (any strong one may decide)
- "Shop by Brand" / "Our Brands A-Z" style navigation listing third-party brands.
- Many distinct third-party brand names across the catalog or homepage.
- Language like "authorized dealer of X", "we carry X, Y, Z", "official
  retailer/stockist of X" — i.e., the company describes itself as selling
  OTHER companies' products.
- The products shown carry brand names that do not match the company/site name.
- shopify_vendor_count of 4 or more (each vendor is a distinct product maker).

BRAND SIGNALS
- "we make / we manufacture / our formula / designed by us / handcrafted in
  our workshop" — first-person creation language.
- Products and site share one brand identity; product names are the company name.
- "Find a retailer / become a dealer / wholesale inquiries" — this means OTHER
  stores sell THEIR product. This is a BRAND-side signal.
- shopify_vendor_count of 0 or 1.

GUARDS — common mistakes you must not make
1. A brand that stocks a FEW complementary third-party items (a candle brand
   selling one brand of matches) is still a brand. Reseller requires the
   multi-brand catalog to be the PRIMARY structure of the store.
2. Direction matters: "become a dealer" (others sell our product) = brand;
   "we are an authorized dealer of X" (we sell X's product) = reseller.
3. Private-label companies that do not manufacture are still brands.
4. Marketing blurbs lie. Weigh the homepage text and structural features over
   the provided third-party description.
5. If the homepage text is thin, generic, or contradictory, return "unknown"
   with low confidence. NEVER guess "brand" to be safe — unsure means unknown.

INPUT
A JSON object per company: company_name, domain, third_party_description
(may be empty or misleading), homepage (extracted title/nav/footer/body text),
and features (shopify_vendor_count, nav_has_shop_by_brand,
reseller_phrase_hits, brand_phrase_hits — computed deterministically; null
means not available).

OUTPUT
Reply with ONLY a JSON object, no prose, no markdown fences:
{
  "label": "brand" | "reseller" | "unknown",
  "confidence": "high" | "medium" | "low",
  "evidence_quote": "<short verbatim quote or feature from the input that most supports the label>",
  "primary_signal": "<one of: vendor_count, shop_by_brand_nav, dealer_language, creation_language, single_identity, thin_evidence, other>"
}
