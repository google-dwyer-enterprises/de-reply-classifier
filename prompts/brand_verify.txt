You are verifying a company for a cold-outreach lead list, using evidence
extracted from its actual website. Judge FIVE things from the page: (1) brand
vs reseller, (2) product category, (3) whether a real DTC store exists,
(4) name consistency, (5) business-model red flags (MLM) and market signals.

== 1. BRAND vs RESELLER ==
- brand: sells products under ITS OWN brand identity. Manufacturing or
  private-labeling both count. One consistent brand identity across the site.
- reseller: the catalog is primarily OTHER companies' brands — a retailer,
  dealer, distributor, or multi-brand shop.
- unknown: insufficient or conflicting evidence.

RESELLER SIGNALS: "Shop by Brand" navigation; many distinct third-party brand
names; "authorized dealer of X" / "we carry X, Y, Z" / "official stockist";
products whose brand names don't match the company/site name;
shopify_vendor_count of 4 or more.
BRAND SIGNALS: "we make / manufacture / our formula" creation language;
products named after the company; "find a retailer / become a dealer /
wholesale inquiries" (means OTHER stores sell THEIR product — brand-side);
shopify_vendor_count of 0 or 1.
GUARDS: a brand stocking a FEW complementary third-party items is still a
brand (multi-brand must be the PRIMARY structure). Direction matters:
"become a dealer" = brand; "we are a dealer of X" = reseller. Private label =
brand. Marketing blurbs lie — weigh page text and structural features over
the third-party description.

== 2. CATEGORY ==
Classify what the company sells and set category_status:
- approved: apparel, cosmetics/skincare, food & beverage, supplements,
  personal care, home & furniture, sporting goods, pet products, consumer
  goods, groceries, electronics, toys — physical consumer products.
- banned: cannabis/hemp/CBD, alcohol, firearms/ammunition, adult/sex toys.
- out_of_scope: books/publishing, software/SaaS, education/courses, real
  estate, insurance, memberships, non-profits, pure services (marketing,
  consulting, salon, spa, gym, clinic, daycare, diagnostics/testing
  subscriptions).
- unclear: can't tell.

== 3. REAL DTC STORE (sells_online) ==
- yes: a working cart/checkout selling to consumers on this site (or an
  obvious linked own storefront).
- no: catalog-only, dealer-locator-only, "find a showroom", quote-request
  B2B sites, service-booking sites — no way for a consumer to buy online.
- unclear.

== 4. NAME CONSISTENCY (name_match) ==
- pass: the brand on the site (logo/title) matches the company name AND the
  domain reasonably.
- mismatch: the site is branded as a DIFFERENT name than the company/domain
  (possible reseller, parent company, or wrong-company match) — explain in
  the evidence.
- unclear.

== 5. BUSINESS MODEL & MARKET ==
- is_mlm: "yes" only for multi-level/direct-sales structure — "join as a
  consultant/distributor", "find your consultant", host rewards, income
  disclosure, downline language. Influencer/ambassador/affiliate marketing
  programs are NOT MLM ("brand ambassador" alone = no). Else "no"/"unclear".
- hq_foreign_signals: "yes" if the page suggests a non-US/Canada home market
  (prices ONLY in £/€/AUD/NZD/INR etc., foreign addresses, .com.au/.co.uk
  canonical links), else "no"/"unclear".
- sells_us_ca: does it sell to US/Canada customers (USD/CAD pricing, US/CA
  shipping, US storefront/retailers)? "yes"/"no"/"unclear". A US-market site
  with $ pricing = yes. Only meaningful when hq_foreign_signals is yes.

If the homepage text is thin, generic, or contradictory, use "unknown"/
"unclear" with low confidence. NEVER guess the favorable answer to be safe.
