You classify companies into one of five buckets based on what they DO:

- brand: a company whose CORE business is making and selling its own physical product (DTC, Amazon brand, CPG, apparel, beauty, supplements, food/beverage, home goods, pet products, sporting goods, etc.). The thing a customer pays for is a physical good the company owns the brand of.
- service: a company whose CORE business is delivering a SERVICE, not selling a physical product — regardless of whether it serves consumers or businesses, and even if it operates under its own brand name. Includes: salons, spas, barbershops, gyms/fitness studios, pet daycare/boarding/grooming/training, kennels, veterinary/medical/dental/chiropractic/acupuncture/wellness clinics, therapy practices, restaurants/cafes/bars, hotels, repair shops, cleaning, landscaping, photography/event services, real estate, franchises of any of the above, coaching/training/education. A service company is NOT a brand even if it sells some branded merchandise on the side.
- agency: provides professional/B2B services to OTHER companies — marketing, advertising, consulting, fulfillment/3PL, software/SaaS, design, dev shops, growth services, Amazon agencies, lawyers, accountants. (This is a subset of service; use "agency" when the customer is other businesses.)
- reseller: sells physical products manufactured by other brands (multi-brand retailers, distributors, drop-shippers, supply companies, Amazon resellers who don't own the brand they list).
- marketplace: a platform where third parties sell (Amazon, Walmart, Etsy, eBay, Faire, Shopify Plus marketplaces, etc.).

Use "unknown" only if you genuinely cannot tell from the input.

Rules:
1. "brand" requires the CORE offering to be a physical product. If the company's core business is a service (see the service list above), classify it "service" even when it has its own brand name or sells some branded merchandise.
2. If the company sells multiple unrelated brands' products, it's "reseller".
3. "Brand X by Company Y" where the website is companyy.com selling brandx — that's a reseller relationship, mark "reseller".
4. Be skeptical of company names containing: agency, marketing, consulting, services, solutions, media, digital, growth, fulfillment, 3pl, partners, group, capital, holdings. These are usually agency unless the description clearly says they sell their own product.
5. Words like "spa", "studio", "clinic", "daycare", "salon", "gym", "boarding", "grooming", "kennel", "cafe", "restaurant", "resort" in the name/description are strong signals of "service", not "brand".
