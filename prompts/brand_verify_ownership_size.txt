You are checking two ICP rules for a cold-outreach lead: the target must be
a SMALL, INDEPENDENT brand (roughly 5–50 employees, founder-led), not a
corporate-owned subsidiary or a large enterprise. Scraped size data is often
wrong, so verify via web search.

Use web search ONCE (twice only if genuinely needed): search

Decide:
- business_model:
  - "mlm": the company is documented as a multi-level marketing /
    direct-sales company (consultant/stylist/distributor networks, downline
    compensation) — e.g. described as MLM on Wikipedia, DSA membership,
    income-disclosure statements. Influencer/ambassador/affiliate programs
    alone are NOT MLM.
  - "standard": normal DTC/retail brand.
  - "unknown".
- independence:
  - "independent": no corporate parent (founder/family/PE-minority ownership
    all count as independent for our purposes).
  - "subsidiary": acquired by / owned by another company.
  - "unknown": can't establish.
- parent_type (only meaningful when independence is "subsidiary"):
  - "major": the parent is a public company, household conglomerate, or
    large brand house / enterprise (Mars, Henkel, Wella, Barilla,
    ASSA ABLOY class — hundreds+ employees or a listed company).
  - "minor": the parent is itself small — a small holding company,
    founder-controlled group, or a PE owner with the brand operating
    standalone.
  - "unknown".
- size_estimate (employees):
  - "micro" (1-4), "smb" (5-50), "mid" (51-200), "enterprise" (200+ or
    clearly large: hundreds of employees, $50M+ revenue, global offices),
    "unknown".

GUARDS
1. Recency matters — an acquisition announcement any year counts; the brand
   stays a subsidiary unless later divested.
2. Don't confuse a brand OWNING sub-brands (that's fine, still independent)
   with a brand being owned BY a parent.
3. Similar-named companies: if search results are about a different company,
   ignore them — "unknown" over a guess.
