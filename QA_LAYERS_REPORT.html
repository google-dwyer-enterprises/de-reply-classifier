Lead Quality: Before & After the Q/A Layers

What we found in the 558-lead BetterContact batch, the verification system we built,
and what it caught — June 10, 2026

1The starting point — your reseller concern was right

In the review call you flagged that some accepted leads looked like resellers — shops selling other companies' products instead of brands selling their own. We investigated and confirmed the root cause: the old filter never looked at any company's website. It judged each lead from a marketing blurb and the company name only. All the evidence that separates a brand from a reseller — the product catalog, the "shop by brand" menus, the dealer language — was on websites nothing ever read.

❌ Before

  • AI filter saw only the company name, URL text, and a marketing description
  • Nothing ever visited the website
  • No second opinion on any verdict
  • No evidence trail — no way to answer "why was this lead accepted?"

✅ After

  • Every company's website is actually checked — catalog, branding, language
  • Four escalating layers, cheapest first; humans only see the genuinely ambiguous
  • Every uncertain or negative verdict gets an independent second check
  • Every verdict carries a quoted piece of evidence

2The Q/A system we built (now runs on every new batch)

FREE

Layer 1 — Hard evidence, no AI

Each company's store catalog is read directly (who makes each product?), and its name is matched against 275,000 registered Amazon brands (registration requires a trademark). Catalog full of other companies' brands → flagged. All products its own → confirmed brand.
▼
~1¢ / company

Layer 2 — Read the website + AI judgment

The homepage is fetched and an AI judges brand-vs-reseller from what's actually on the page — and must quote the evidence. Unsure never passes silently; it escalates.
▼
~3¢ / company

Layer 3 — Web research for the hard cases

Sites that block automated reading get judged from their public footprint (LinkedIn, Amazon, press) via web search. Catalogs that look like resellers get an ownership check — are those vendor names other companies, or this company's own product lines? (This caught the difference between a real reseller and, say, Vetnique — whose "vendors" Glandex and Dermabliss are its own brands.)
▼
HUMAN

Layer 4 — Anything still ambiguous goes to a human

Flagged leads appear in the review page with the evidence attached — seconds per decision instead of minutes of digging. Nothing ambiguous is ever auto-passed or auto-rejected.
Accuracy was measured before anything shipped. The system was tested against 60 known resellers and the full accepted pool, twice, before going live: the AI site-check produced zero false reseller flags across both test runs, and its measured catch rate on judged known-resellers was 75% — with most "misses" turning out to be companies the old filter had wrongly rejected (BDI Furniture, Butler Specialty, Diamond Cosmetics are real brands).

4What this means for future batches

The reseller-detection layers now run automatically inside every new scrape — each company is judged by the cheapest method that can decide confidently, every verdict is cached (a company is never re-researched), and the review page shows a Brand check column with the evidence on hover. Expected steady-state: ~96–98% of leads decided automatically with ~90%+ reseller catch rate, for roughly $50–80/month at 20,000 leads/month.
