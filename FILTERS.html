🔎 Filters Reference

Every filter the lead scraper applies, in the order it fires

The scraper applies nine layers of filtering. Each layer removes the obvious junk so the next layer (which is more expensive) only sees high-quality candidates.

1CSV cleanup — junk domains

1Pre-processing — runs once on the raw CSV

When: before the bot ever starts · Script: scripts/clean_inclusion.py

Looks at each row's Website and Emails column. Drops the row if either points to a junk domain.

RuleExamplesWhy drop
Ends in .orgredcross.org, wikipedia.orgNon-profits — not e-com brands
Ends in .eduharvard.edu, mit.eduUniversities

Result on real data:

  • inclusion.csv: 130,127 → 99,809 rows (dropped 23%)
  • exclusion.csv: 82,675 → 76,678 rows (dropped 7%)

1bMega-brand blocklist

When: same pre-processing pass · Script: scripts/clean_inclusion.py · Set: MEGA_BRANDS

Drops rows pointing to ~80 Fortune-500-class domains. Two reasons:

  • They're not real targets. Adidas, Pfizer, Ford have in-house teams and don't buy from Amazon-agency outreach.
  • They break Prospeo's batched filter. A single mega-brand in a 15-domain batch returns 100+ exec-titled rows, swamping the small brands in the batch and triggering our safety check. (This was a real bug — discovered after a 506-credit burn.)

Examples blocked: adidas.com, amazon.com, walmart.com, nestle.com, pg.com, pfizer.com, tesla.com, google.com, and ~70 more. Also catches consumer brands owned by mega-corps (e.g. cheetos.com is blocked because its emails point to pepsico.com).

Result: 74 unique mega-brand domains removed from inclusion_clean.csv → inclusion_clean_v2.csv.

2Dedupe filters — don't re-pay for what we have

2Skip domains where we already have a decision-maker

When: bot startup, before any API call · Source: lead_contacts table

If our database already contains a Founder, CEO, Owner, President, CMO, or Head-of-anything at brandX.com — skip brandX.com entirely. No point asking Prospeo to find a CEO we already have.

3Skip domains we've already scraped recently

When: bot startup · Source: domain_inclusion_list.last_scraped_at · Window: 30 days

Every batch the bot completes marks its 15 domains as scraped in domain_inclusion_list. The next run filters those out at load time — so re-runs only hit fresh domains, and a crashed run resumes automatically. After 30 days a domain becomes "stale" and eligible for re-scraping (brands change owners, new founders join).

4Skip emails we already have anywhere

When: after Prospeo returns a person, before saving · Source: lead_contacts + replies + prospeo_new_leads

Even if a new domain returns a fresh lead, we double-check the email against everywhere we've seen one before. If the email exists anywhere — drop it. Prevents contacting the same person via two different campaigns.

3Prospeo search filter — job titles only

5Owner-level titles, server-side filtering

When: sent to Prospeo with every batch of 15 domains · Endpoint: POST /search-person

This is the most important filter. Prospeo applies it on their side — we never even see leads that don't match a target title, so we don't pay credits to look at them.

CEO Chief Executive Officer Founder Owner Co-Owner President Founder and CEO Managing Director Chairman

Prospeo's Smart match mode also auto-expands these to close variants like "Founding Partner", "Co-CEO", "Acting President" — we don't have to list every spelling ourselves.

Why owner-level only?

Looked at every classified reply in Dwyer's Supabase. Marketing-titled people (CMO, Marketing Director, Head of E-com) produced 1 booking out of 15 replies. Founder/CEO/Owner/President produced 22 positives out of ~60. Your data, not industry guesswork.

The actual API call looks like this:

{
  "filters": {
    "company": { "websites": { "include": [] } },
    "company_revenue": { "min": "500K" },
    "person_job_title": {
      "include": ["CEO", "Founder", "Owner", "President", ...],
      "match": "smart",
      "match_strictness": "normal"
    }
  }
}

5bMinimum annual revenue — $500K floor

When: sent to Prospeo alongside the title filter · Endpoint: POST /search-person · Constant: PROSPEO_MIN_REVENUE

Drops companies whose annual revenue Prospeo estimates below $500,000. Server-side — we never see (or pay for) the smaller brands.

Why a revenue floor?

Sub-$500K brands rarely have the budget to engage a paid agency. Filtering them out at the Prospeo layer means fewer credits spent on enrich-person calls for prospects we wouldn't pursue anyway. Applied to both domain and category modes.

The value format took empirical work

Prospeo accepts a string shorthand under company_revenue.min — "500K", "1M", "10M". Numeric ints (500000), dollar-prefixed strings ("$1M"), comparator strings ("$1M+"), and bucket-list .include shapes either 400 with INVALID_FILTERS or silently no-op (return unchanged total_count). Verified 2026-05-21 via scripts/probe_revenue_filter.py — about $0.30 of credits to pin down.

Bench check: on a Cosmetics + owner-titles baseline of 4,024 candidates, the 500K floor reduces the search to 612 — an ~85% cut before we spend a single enrich credit.

4Verified-email filter — protect deliverability

6Only pay for verified emails

When: every enrich call · Endpoint: POST /enrich-person

Tell Prospeo only_verified_email: true. If they don't have an independently-verified email for the person, they return 400/NO_MATCH and we skip silently. Zero credits spent.

Why this is non-negotiable

Cold email lives or dies on sender reputation. A hard bounce in Instantly damages the sending domain across all campaigns — not just the one that bounced. Saving credits on unverified addresses is a bonus; deliverability is the real win.

5Agency / marketplace rules — fast & deterministic

7Drop obvious agencies and marketplaces

When: after enrich, before LLM · Code: rule_classify()

Some companies are obviously not brands. Two cheap rule checks catch the most common cases:

6a · Marketplace rejection

If the email domain or website is in our marketplace list, the lead is rejected as marketplace:

amazon.com walmart.com etsy.com ebay.com faire.com shopify.com alibaba.com aliexpress.com

6b · Agency keyword rejection

If the company name OR website contains any of these tokens, reject as agency:

agency marketing consulting consultancy services solutions media digital growth fulfillment 3pl partners advisors advisory

If neither rule fires, the lead is grey-zone — uncertain enough that we send it to the LLM for a second opinion.

6LLM grey-zone — Claude Haiku decides

8AI judgment on the borderline cases

When: leads that passed all rules but couldn't be classified · Model: Claude Haiku 4.5

For every lead the rules couldn't decide, Haiku reads the company's name, website, description, the person's title, and the email domain, then returns one of:

brand — real e-commerce company selling their own product (kept)
agency — provides services to other companies
reseller — sells products other brands own
marketplace — multi-vendor platform
unknown — Haiku couldn't tell with confidence

Only brand is accepted for export. Everything else is saved to prospeo_new_leads with rejected=true for audit, but not exported to the CSV.

Cost: ~$0.0001 per grey-zone lead. Negligible.

7The full funnel

Raw inclusion CSV 130,127 rows ↓ CSV cleanup (.org/.edu/.io/freemail dropped) 99,809 rows ↓ mega-brand blocklist (~80 Fortune-500 domains dropped) 99,142 rows (74 unique mega-brand domains removed) ↓ dedupe on Website column 51,681 unique company domains (v2) ↓ skip domains where we already have a decision-maker ↓ skip domains already scraped in the last 30 days ~remaining new domains queried ↓ Prospeo title filter (owner-level only) ~5–10% return matches ↓ Prospeo revenue floor ($500K minimum) ~85% of title matches survive (sub-$500K dropped) ↓ Prospeo verified-email filter ~30–40% of matches have a verified email ↓ marketplace / agency rules obvious junk rejected ↓ Haiku LLM grey-zone subtler agencies rejected ↓ ACCEPTED BRAND LEADS ~500–1,500 from a full run

Why so many filter layers?

Each layer is cheaper than the next. CSV cleanup is free. Database dedupe is free. Prospeo's server-side filter is one credit per page. Email verification only charges if it works. The LLM only sees borderline cases. By the time we spend real money, we're confident the candidate is worth checking.

8Filters we considered but DON'T use

⏳ Industry filter — being re-evaluated

Originally rejected because Prospeo's taxonomy includes agencies and marketplaces in categories like "Retail Apparel." That reasoning still holds in principle, but our existing agency/marketplace LLM filter is designed to clean exactly that kind of pollution — so the question is whether it cleans enough at category-mode scale.

Status: Victor flagged the 52k domain ceiling as a blocker for the 5,000-leads/week target. A side-by-side pilot (scripts/prospeo_category_pilot.py) is queued — it runs a Domain pass and a Category pass under the same downstream filter and compares brand-rate. Filter shape and the verified industry-string list have been empirically confirmed (FINDINGS.html §6). Awaiting authorization to spend ~$0.80 on the live pilot.

❌ Reseller-by-email-mismatch rule

We tried flagging leads where the email domain differed from the company website (e.g. @atd1.net for americantoolanddie.com). Too many false positives — many real brands use legacy/short email domains. Removed.

❌ Seniority filter

Earlier version used Prospeo's seniority buckets (C-Suite, Vice President, etc.). Too broad — "Vice President" includes VP of Engineering, Finance, Operations. Switched to specific job titles with Smart match.

❌ Employee count filter

Available on Prospeo but not enabled — would narrow supply further on top of the strict title + revenue filters. Can enable later if quality still overshoots. (Country is exposed via the --country flag in category mode; the $500K revenue floor is now active — see stage 5b above.)
