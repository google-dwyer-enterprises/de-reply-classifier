Reading the two rows: the worst-case savings are larger only because the before baseline is larger (11.6 vs 4.16 cr/lead) — you save what you were wasting, and stale segments wasted more. The $2,540/mo is the magnitude of the fix on bad segments, not a typical month; the recurring saving to budget for is the steady-state ~$450/mo.

On the credit price: dollars use BetterContact's live published pricing (verified June 2026): Starter $15 / 200 cr = $0.075, Pro $49 / 1,000 cr = $0.049, Enterprise from $799 for 50k+ cr ≈ $0.016/cr (exact Enterprise pricing is custom/undisclosed; 15–30% agency discounts apply above 5,000 cr/mo). The credit counts are exact; only the $/credit carries this band. Two stale internal notes — a $0.20/cr comment in worker.py and a $0.02/cr one in submit.html — were 10× apart; the live Enterprise rate confirms ~$0.016, i.e. submit.html was right.

Plus the silent riders: the AI-gate cache and 6-way parallel calls cut token spend and bring a 5k-lead weekly batch's verification from a projected 5–6 hours to ~1–1.5 hours.

The one-sentence change: the pipeline used to pay first and filter later — now every lead is rejected by the cheapest stage able to reject it, and as much rejection as possible happens server-side, before BetterContact bills us. Same leads, same quality gates, money spent only on candidates that have a real chance of surviving.

Two myths the measurements killed along the way: deep search pages do not decay (healthy segments were flat at 3.3–5.1 credits/accepted at any depth — the expensive runs came from exhausted segments, which is a different problem with a different fix); and "leads without an email are free" is false — probe billing showed BetterContact charges for found-but-undeliverable emails (catch-alls), now raised with their support.

3The resequencing, side by side

❌ Before — pay, then filter

BC search (industries, headcount) → BC BILLS for every found email → drop no-email rows → dedup vs our DB (paid waste) → TLD/size/category/language rules → wrong-title check (paid waste) → AI gate, per CONTACT, serial, no cache (paid + token waste) → 3-per-company cap (after the AI gate!) → website-verification funnel → human review → MV gate → pool

✅ After — filter, then pay

SERVER-SIDE, before billing: • suppression list: 500 known/capped/ rejected companies excluded • title excludes (sales/finance/HR VPs…) • segment auto-park when yield dies → BC bills (for a far cleaner stream) → local rules (unchanged, as backstop) → 3-per-company cap (moved before AI) → AI gate per COMPANY, cached across runs, 6-way parallel → website-verification funnel (unchanged) → human review → MV gate → pool

Residual duplicates are by design: companies still under the 3-contact cap are deliberately not suppressed (we want their remaining contacts), so same-email repeats there still get deduped locally.

Evaluated and deliberately NOT shipped: the Prospeo "search-then-enrich" hybrid. A $15 pilot measured it honestly — inventory is ample (60,998 in-ICP people) and email quality holds (94% deliverability- verified), but only 52% of enriched emails are deliverable and BetterContact bills the rest, so the real saving is ~25–35% more, not the 60–70% the documentation implied. Deferred — the recorded trigger to revisit is BetterContact's answer on deliverable-only billing, which would roughly double the hybrid's value. Also dropped: two micro-optimizations whose quality risk or premise didn't survive re-verification.
What did not change — by construction: lead quality. Server-side filters only reduce what we pay for, never what we accept: every local rule still runs as a backstop, the website-verification funnel, review queue, and MillionVerifier gate are untouched, and the per-company AI verdict is provably more consistent than the old per-contact coin flips. The leads reaching Victor and Jam are identical or better — they just cost a third as much.
