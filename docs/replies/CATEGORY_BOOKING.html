How to read this — and what it can't tell you.
  • Descriptive, not proof. It shows where bookings happened, not that a category causes bookings. Volume matters too — check the "share of bookings" before chasing a high rate on a small base.
  • Coverage: 353 of 483 bookings (73%) have a category. 89 were recovered by borrowing their company-domain's category; the remaining 130 (mostly leads whose reply address isn't in the enrichment pool) stay uncategorized and are excluded from the ranking.
  • Confidence: categories under 30 engaged leads aren't ranked (shown as counts only); every ranked rate carries a Wilson 95% interval — wide intervals mean "not enough data to trust the order yet."
  • Domain borrow assumption: leads on the same company domain are treated as the same category — safe for single-brand companies, looser for conglomerates.
  • Category labels come from enrichment and mix Amazon browse categories (which dominate the ranked list) with a long tail of other industry labels; only categories with real volume are ranked.
