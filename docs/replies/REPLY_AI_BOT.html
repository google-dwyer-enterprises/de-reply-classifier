Reply AI Bot — how it works

Plain-English guide   What the cold-reply analysis does, what each status means, and the two newest pieces — the booked-count fix and the "which follow-ups are working" report. Snapshot: 2026-06-15.

1. What it does, in one breath

Every day, people reply to the cold emails we send. The Reply AI Bot reads each reply, decides what the person actually meant (are they booking a call? not interested? out of office?), and shows that on a simple per-lead status board in NocoDB. Nobody has to read thousands of replies by hand — and the sales team's own judgement is never overwritten.

It refreshes on a schedule (daily, early morning Central time), so the board stays current without anyone pressing a button.

2. The 11 statuses, in plain words

Each reply is sorted into exactly one of these. A lead's headline status is its strongest signal.

StatusWhat it means
bookedA meeting or call is scheduled — the win.
interestedWants to keep talking now — asks for pricing, a deck, or a next step.
interested_pastWas keen earlier, then the conversation went quiet.
not_nowA soft "not yet" tied to timing — circle back later. Door's still open.
not_interestedA clear "no" — not a fit, or they chose someone else.
wrong_person"That's not me" — points to a colleague instead.
no_longer_thereThey've left the company / the role is gone.
unsubscribe"Take me off the list."
customer_serviceA support/billing question — not a sales reply.
oofAn out-of-office auto-reply.
otherDoesn't fit anything above (e.g. a newsletter, or not in English).

The sales team always wins

If someone on the team has set a status by hand on a lead, the bot never overwrites it. The AI only fills in the leads nobody has touched.

3. What's new (1): the booked count is no longer under-reported

The problem: when a rep books a meeting, the confirming reply often reads like a casual "great, talk then!" — which the AI would file as merely interested. Meanwhile the rep had already tagged that lead "Booked" inside Instantly. The board trusted the AI's reading of the text and undercounted the real bookings.

The fix: the rep's "Booked" / "Interested" tag in Instantly is now treated as the source of truth and lifts the lead's status to match. It can only ever raise a status, never lower one — so a lead the AI already marked booked can't be knocked down by a weaker tag.

Before

tagged-booked leads sat under "interested" or "other"
After
449 booked
+54 leads the team had already tagged "Booked" now count correctly

Each promoted lead also says why, e.g. Status set from Instantly sales tag 'Epic - Booked', so it's transparent — and the detailed "Status 1/2/3" columns still show the AI's own reading underneath.

4. What's new (2): which follow-ups are actually working?

When a first reply comes in, reps often send manual follow-up messages by hand from the Instantly inbox. This new report looks across all of them and asks: after a follow-up like this, did a good reply come back?

It then breaks the follow-ups down by their characteristics and shows the positive-reply rate with versus without each one. Two kinds of characteristic:

  • Plain facts (rules): length, whether it asks a question, includes a booking link, mentions pricing, and so on.
  • AI-judged style (new): an AI also reads each message and tags its hook (question / stat / compliment / value-prop / reminder…), tone (casual / formal / neutral), call-to-action (soft / direct / permission-based / none), and personalization (none / light / deep). These appear in the report as the "… (AI)" rows. They're judgement calls, not exact facts — read them as clues, same as everything else.

The full report lives at FOLLOWUP_EFFECTIVENESS.html, and the same tables are available as live views in NocoDB.

⚠️ How to read it honestly (please read this)

  • It shows patterns, not proof. "Shorter messages got more replies" is an association — it does not prove that making a message shorter causes more replies.
  • Most follow-ups go to already-warm leads. A lot of these messages are gentle nudges to people who were already interested, so high reply rates there are expected — that's flagged in the report as its own row ("Lead Already Positive") rather than hidden.
  • "Positive" means the next reply was booked or interested.
  • It's a first read, not the whole story. It's built from one mailbox's sent history so far, so treat it as a starting point for ideas to test — not a rulebook.
