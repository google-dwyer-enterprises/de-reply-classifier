The optimized follow-up playbook

For leads who've shown interest — the most reply-getting way to follow up, FU1 through FU5, ready to copy. Built from 3,562 real follow-ups (335 positive replies, 117 booked). Generated 2026-06-19.

How to follow up — the rules in one place

  • Open with a compliment or the value — never a gimmick. Replies that opened with a specific compliment landed 23% vs 9%; value-prop openers 17% vs 8%. The GIF / “pattern-interrupt” style did the opposite (3.5% vs 11%).
  • Always end with ONE direct ask + your calendar link. A direct ask beat soft/no-ask (11.5% vs 7.7%), and messages that included a booking link replied at 14% vs 9% without one.
  • Keep the tone businesslike, not overly casual. Neutral/formal tone outperformed casual (11-13% vs 8%). Confident and warm, not chatty.
  • Aim for a short paragraph — not a one-liner, not a wall. Very-short one-liners underperformed (7.4%); medium length read best. Give enough to be concrete.
  • Name the concrete model / value. Messages that mentioned the actual offer or pricing structure replied at 17% vs 8%.
  • Front-load the effort. Reply rate falls fast by stage (#1 19% → #3 10% → #6+ under 4%). The first three touches earn most of the bookings — make them count and don't drag the sequence out.

The 5-stage sequence

Use {first_name} / {company} for the lead's details and fill the [bracketed] bits per lead. One ask + your calendar link every time.

FU1
Right after they reply interested
Convert the interest into a booked call while it's hot.
Compliment / hook: A specific compliment about their Amazon store, then the value. Tone: Warm but businesslike. Structure: Compliment → value → direct ask + link. 
Hi {first_name}, thanks for getting back to me. I took a proper look at {company}'s Amazon store — [one specific, genuine compliment, e.g. “your top products are clearly resonating”]. The reason I reached out: brands like yours usually have real untapped demand in the EU, UK and other marketplaces, and we run that expansion end-to-end — we buy your inventory at wholesale and you keep full control of pricing and brand. Worth 15 minutes to map where the biggest wins are? Here's my calendar link.

Why this works: Compliment hook (lift 2.7) + value prop (2.3) + direct CTA with a calendar link (1.5 / booking-link 14%).

FU2
2–3 days later, no reply
Make the offer concrete so it's easy to say yes.
Compliment / hook: Lead with how it actually works. Tone: Neutral, confident. Structure: Value/how-it-works → direct ask with two time options. 
Hi {first_name}, following up on the international expansion idea for {company}. Quick version of how it works: we purchase your inventory at an agreed wholesale price and run the marketplaces (Amazon EU/UK, Walmart, and more) for you — you keep full control of pricing, presentation and brand. Are you free for a 15-minute call Tuesday or Wednesday? Here's my calendar link.

Why this works: Names the concrete model (mentions-model 17% vs 8%), direct ask with options, calendar link.

FU3
3–4 days later
Pre-empt the most common objection (cost / effort).
Compliment / hook: Remove the risk up front. Tone: Neutral, reassuring. Structure: Objection handle → value → direct ask + link. 
Hi {first_name}, I know a new channel can feel like a lift, so to be clear: there's no monthly retainer and no extra fees — we make our margin on the resale, so we only win when {company} wins. If growth in new markets is on your radar this quarter, a short call is the fastest way to see if it's a fit. Here's my calendar link.

Why this works: Pricing/model clarity (mentions-pricing 17% vs 8%) + direct CTA. This is the last 'full-pitch' touch before reply rates drop.

FU4
4–5 days later
Lower the friction to almost zero.
Compliment / hook: Offer something useful + exact times. Tone: Brief, helpful. Structure: Specific value → two concrete times → link. 
Hi {first_name}, still happy to show you what international expansion could look like for {company} specifically — I'll bring a quick analysis of which markets fit best. Does Thursday at 1pm or Friday at 11am (your time) work? Here's my calendar link if another slot is easier.

Why this works: Direct, specific times + a concrete reason to meet. Reply rate here is ~6-7% — keep it short and easy.

FU5
About a week later — the last one
Get a yes/no and close the loop respectfully.
Compliment / hook: A straight priority check. Tone: Direct, low-pressure. Structure: Priority check → permission to close → link if yes. 
Hi {first_name}, I don't want to keep crowding your inbox — is expanding {company} into new marketplaces still a priority right now? If now isn't the time, just say so and I'll close the loop. If it is, here's my calendar link and I'll take it from there.

Why this works: A clear ask still beats a soft fade. After this, reply rates fall under 4% — stop or restart fresh later.

⚠️ How sure are we about this?

These patterns are descriptive, not proven cause-and-effect: most of the analyzed follow-ups went to leads who were already warm, so a style's score is partly who got it, not just how it was written. Notably, this playbook leans toward compliment/value openers, a clear ask and a businesslike tone — and away from the GIF-heavy, ultra-casual style currently in heavy use, because that's what the data favors.

The live A/B test (your curated templates vs AI-written, on the Follow-ups page) is what will prove which actually books more. Treat this as the strong starting hypothesis — and update it as the A/B fills in.
