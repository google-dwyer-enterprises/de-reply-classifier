What it does — your spec, as built

  • Check 1 — On Amazon? Searches Amazon for the brand and counts listings that genuinely belong to it (strict matching — "Blue Apple Co." never gets credited with Apple's products).
  • Check 2 — Revenue ≥ $300k/yr? Exactly the method you described in the meeting: add up per-listing sales — using Amazon's own "bought in past month" labels × price. It under-counts by design, so when the bot says KEEP, that revenue is real. (Threshold lowered from $500k to $300k on Jul 7 per Jam.)
  • Verdicts: KEEP (in ICP) · DROP (not on Amazon / too small) · REVIEW (a small human queue — never silently dropped).
  • The "weird names" problem you predicted ("Anchor" vs "Anchor Electronics Inc") is handled: the bot detects Amazon's own spelling of the brand and re-searches with it. This recovered Scotts Protein Balls ($44M) and Dae Hair ($18M) that a simpler bot would have wrongly dropped.

Note — a search costs 1 credit whether or not there's a match. Rainforest bills per search, not per hit: a company that turns out to be DROP ("not on Amazon", 0 listings) costs the same 1 credit as a KEEP. You're paying to ask the question, not for a positive answer — which is why the free filters (SmartScout, the cache, and the upstream QA gates) run first, so we only spend a credit on companies worth asking about.
