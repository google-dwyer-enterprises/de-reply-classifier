1The problem we're solving

Today

Jam manually pulls leads from Prospeo's website. Filters by hand. Reviews one at a time. Loads into Instantly.

Cycle time: ~3 weeks per drop. We can't keep the pipeline full.

With the bot

One command pulls thousands of decision-maker leads per week. Filters out agencies/resellers automatically. Hands Jam a clean CSV ready to load.

Target: 5,000 verified leads / week.

What Cold IQ does (our competitor)

Cold IQ runs the same pipeline as a service — they charge clients for it. We're building our own version so we own the process and the data.

2Preparing the lists — how we cleaned the data

Before the bot ever talks to Prospeo, we ran the inclusion and exclusion CSVs through a one-time cleanup script. It removes rows where the company website or email belongs to a domain we don't want to target.

What gets thrown out

TypeExamplesWhy drop
.org domainsredcross.org, wikipedia.orgNon-profits — not e-commerce brands.
.edu domainsharvard.edu, mit.eduUniversities.
.io domainsdagster.io, snowplow.ioMostly tech/SaaS, not our market.
Free email providersgmail.com, yahoo.com, outlook.comPersonal addresses, not businesses.

How a single row gets checked

Take this row from the raw file:

Company: Red Cross
Website: https://www.redcross.org/about-us
Emails: info@redcross.org

The script strips it down step by step:

  1. Start with https://www.redcross.org/about-us
  2. Strip https:// → www.redcross.org/about-us
  3. Strip everything after / → www.redcross.org
  4. Strip www. → redcross.org
  5. Check: ends in .org? Yes → drop the row.

Even if the Emails column was clean, one blocked field is enough. Both columns are checked.

Why inclusion is dirtier than exclusion

The inclusion list was scraped from multiple sources over time — lots of junk slipped in (23%). The exclusion list is much cleaner (7%) because it's people we've already successfully emailed through Instantly. Bad addresses got filtered out naturally on the way in.

Why 99,809 rows becomes 52,072 domains

Two counts measure two different things:

CountWhat it represents
99,809 rowsNumber of people in the cleaned CSV (name, title, email per row).
52,072 unique domainsNumber of companies — what you get when you collapse rows by their Website column.

The CSV has multiple rows per company. Example:

1byone, , ushelp@1byone.com, https://1byone.com/...
1byone, Aaron Hu, aaron@1byone.com, https://1byone.com/...

Same website (1byone.com), two rows. The bot loads only the Website column, normalizes it (https://1byone.com/... → 1byone.com), and removes duplicates. Both rows collapse into one domain to query.

Why the bot only needs domains

Prospeo's API works at the company level. We ask "Who are the decision-makers at 1byone.com?" — not "Tell me about Aaron Hu." Asking the same domain twice wastes credits. So 99k rows of people → 52k unique companies → that's what we actually pay to scrape.

The pipeline from cleaned file to API call:

99,809 rows in inclusion_clean.csv ↓ (deduplicate on Website column) 52,072 unique company domains ↓ (subtract domains where we already have a Founder/CEO/etc.) ~50,000 domains the bot actually queries

What it does NOT do

  • Doesn't deduplicate — the bot itself deduplicates downstream.
  • Doesn't validate that emails actually work — Prospeo's verified-email check handles that.
  • Doesn't modify the original file — writes a new _clean.csv alongside, so we can always re-run with different rules.

The script is one tiny file (scripts/clean_inclusion.py, ~40 lines) and runs in a few seconds on both files combined.

4Why this approach

Decision 1: Use our domain list instead of Prospeo's industry filter

Jam manually picks "Retail Apparel and Fashion" in Prospeo's UI. But that category pulls in apparel agencies, fulfillment centers, and marketplaces — junk we have to filter out anyway.

Our 52k domain list is already curated by Jam. Every domain on it is a real brand. Starting there is faster and cleaner.

Decision 2: Verified emails only

Cold email lives or dies on sender reputation. Hard bounces destroy it. Prospeo lets us toggle "only verified" — we use it. The credits we save are a bonus; the real win is deliverability across all our Instantly campaigns.

Decision 3: Auto-filter agencies and resellers

Even within our curated list, the bot finds the occasional agency or service company that snuck in. Claude Haiku reads the company description and decides — costs nothing meaningful, catches what rules miss.

5The big finding — we trust your data over textbook advice

🧠 What the textbook says

Generic lead-gen wisdom: target CEOs AND CMOs, Marketing Directors, Heads of E-commerce. Mix of authority and specialty.

📊 What your reply data actually shows

We looked at every reply you've classified in Supabase — all 17,376 of them, with Wilson 95% confidence intervals so we know which differences are real. Marketing-titled people convert about 3× worse than Founder-level.

Title groupPositive replies95% CIPattern
Founder / CEO / Owner / President 927 / 6,335 → 14.6% [13.8, 15.5] Where the great majority of bookings and "interested" replies come from.
CMO / Marketing Director / Head of E-com 28 / 538 → 5.2% [3.6, 7.4] Still profitable but ~3× lower; lots of "wrong person", "no longer there", out-of-office. They forward to the Founder.
COO / Other-Chief / VP / Director 47 / 1,058 → 4.4% [3.4, 5.9] Wrong audience.
Generic Manager / Specialist / Other 112 / 1,736 → 6.5% [5.4, 7.7] Surprisingly competitive with CMOs, but still well below owners.

Why this matters

Your clients are mostly small/mid DTC brands. The Owner IS the marketing decision-maker. Even at bigger brands, the CMO often doesn't have agency-spend authority — they forward to the Founder anyway. That's why marketing-titled leads come back as "wrong person."

So we filter Prospeo to Owner-level titles only as the primary pass. CMO is reasonable as a fallback at brands where Prospeo has no Founder listed — but is not the priority lead.

An earlier version of this section used "22 / ~60" and "1 / 15" numbers based on a 166-row subset (v3 prompt-version only) — the 95% CI on the 5-row CMO sample was [3.6%, 62.4%], too wide to be decisive. The numbers above are re-derived from the full 17,376-reply corpus. See FINDINGS.html §2 for the methodology.

The final title list

These are the only job titles the bot asks Prospeo for. Everything else gets filtered out at the source — we don't pay credits to find Marketing VPs or generic Managers anymore.

CEO Chief Executive Officer Founder Owner Co-Owner President Founder and CEO Managing Director Chairman

Prospeo's "Smart match" expands these — so "Founding Partner", "Co-CEO", "Acting President", etc. get pulled in automatically without us having to list every variant.

Recommendation

Use email-only for routine scraping. Add mobile only to the leads Atlas will actually cold-call — usually a small subset of the accepted list, after Jam reviews and prioritizes. The catch-up command (enrich-mobile) can be run any time on demand.

Reality check on the 5,000 leads/week target

At our strict Owner-only filter, expect ~150 verified leads per 5,000 domains queried. The full inclusion list yields somewhere around 500–1,500 verified Owner-level leads, period. To hit 5,000/week consistently we'd need to either:

  • Loosen the title filter (lowers quality — we'd be paying for replies that don't convert), or
  • Expand the domain list beyond the current 52k (waterfall to Find-the-Email, Apollo, etc. — Cold IQ's strategy)

Recommendation: ship the high-quality 500–1,500 first, prove the conversion rate, then decide whether to chase volume.
