Which AI provider should each task use — and what would it cost?

Our system uses AI for several jobs (sorting replies, checking brands, cleaning data, filtering leads). Today that all runs on one provider, Anthropic. We tested whether OpenAI or Google Gemini could do the same jobs as well — and what each would cost per month at our volumes (20,000 leads/month, plus the reply traffic). We tested every tier of each: from the cheapest models right up to OpenAI's GPT-5.5 and Gemini's 3.1 Pro, to see both whether we can save money and whether paying more buys better quality. Generated 2026-06-17.

The 60-second summary

  • The reply-side jobs cost almost nothing on any provider (well under $1/month) — not worth changing for cost.
  • Cleaning up company names is a free upgrade: OpenAI's cheapest model is actually a bit more accurate than what we use today, and far cheaper. Worth switching.
  • Filtering scraped leads should stay as-is — the cheaper models make noticeably more mistakes, and the saving is tiny.
  • Brand-checking is the only big cost, but most of it is an unavoidable web-search fee that's the same on every provider — so switching providers barely helps there. Don't rebuild it just to save money.
  • Paying for higher-tier models doesn't help — with one exception. The pricier OpenAI/Gemini models (up to GPT-5.5 and Gemini 3.1 Pro) cost several times more and are no more accurate on most tasks. The lone exception is the lead filter, where Gemini 3.1 Pro is clearly better (92% vs 82%) — but ~6× the cost.

Bottom line: take the free company-name win; leave everything else where it is. Going to a fancier model doesn't pay off (except, optionally, the lead filter). The big-looking numbers below are mostly a search fee no provider can avoid, not a saving you can capture by switching.

Short answer: mostly no — with one real exception.
  • On 4 of the 5 tasks, paying more buys nothing. For sorting replies, tagging follow-ups, and cleaning company names, the higher tiers match or do slightly worse than today while costing several times more — bigger "reasoning" models over-think these short, format-locked jobs. On company names the cheapest models (GPT-5.4 nano 92%, Gemini Flash-Lite 90%) already beat today's Haiku (88%), so there's no reason to climb.
  • The one exception is the lead filter. This is the task where cheap models trail (Haiku 82%; nano 64%, Flash-Lite 74%). Here the high tiers do help: GPT-5.5 reaches 84% and Gemini 3.1 Pro reaches 92% (+10 points over today). But it's expensive — on this task alone Pro runs ~$128/mo vs $22 today (~6×, +$106/mo) and GPT-5.5 ~$100/mo (+$78/mo) — for a +2 to +10 point accuracy gain.
  • Brand-checking only gets pricier. Higher tiers cost $260–660/mo on the model alone (vs ~$121 today) — never worth it.
Bottom line: no higher tier is worth a blanket switch. The only place paying more is even arguable is the lead filter — and only if its accuracy matters enough to justify ~$80–106/mo extra. Everything else stays put; the cheap-tier recommendation (nano for company names) is unchanged.

The big one — checking if a company is a real brand

This is where almost all the money is (~$121.61/mo of AI cost at 20,000 leads/month, plus search fees — below). We checked whether every other model — cheap and premium — can do its core judgment as well as our current one, using 56 companies a human had already graded.

  • Quality holds across the board. Every model we tested agreed with our current verdicts 96%–100% of the time and — most important — none wrongly rejected a good company (0 false rejections in total, same clean record as today out of 30 good companies).
  • But switching barely saves money — and going premium costs far more. Most of brand-checking's cost is the web-search fee — about $190–255/month at 20,000 leads — and that fee is almost the same on every provider ($10–14 per 1,000 searches). Changing the AI model only trims the smaller "thinking" cost; the cheap models trim it (~$70–90/mo of model cost), but the higher tiers multiply it ($260–660/mo on the model alone vs ~$121 today).
  • Recommendation: don't rebuild brand-checking on another provider just to save money — the saving is small and it's ~3 days of work to rebuild + maintain, and no premium model is worth its cost here. If brand-checking cost matters, the real lever is doing fewer web searches, which is a separate change.

Quality was measured on the no-tool site-verdict step (the portable core of the funnel); the web-search steps are provider-specific and were not re-implemented. Gemini 3.1 Pro is excluded from this quality test — its forced "thinking" on full website payloads runs many minutes per call, prohibitive for a 20k-lead/month step (and its brand-verify cost alone, ~$663/mo, already rules it out). Gemini 3.5 Flash was rate-limited by Google (503s), leaving a small sample. Both are shown by their company counts in the table.

Would "caching" cut the cost? (we checked — no)

Providers offer prompt caching: if you send the exact same instructions over and over, you pay ~90% less for that repeated part after the first time. It sounds like an easy saving, so we tested whether it helps us.

  • It's already switched on for two tasks — but it does nothing. Reply-sorting and follow-up tagging have a cache marker in the code, but we measured it live: zero cache hits. Anthropic only caches instructions above ~4,000 tokens; ours are ~1,600 or smaller, so they fall under the cutoff and silently don't cache.
  • The other tasks have no caching — and their instructions are even smaller, so turning it on would also do nothing.
  • Even if it worked, it only discounts the AI "thinking" cost — the small part. It can't touch the brand-checking web-search fee, which is where the real money is.

Bottom line: caching is a non-lever for us — realistic saving ≈ $0. The existing markers are harmless, so we leave them. (The only case where caching would matter is moving a high-volume task to OpenAI, whose caching starts at a smaller size and would shave a little off the token cost — still minor, and not worth a switch on its own.)

  • ⚠️ The one estimated piece — brand-checking's web-search fee: how many searches per month is an assumption (not yet measured at 20k-lead scale), and only brand-checking's core judgment was quality-tested, not its web-search steps.

So: outside the brand-checking web-search line, treat these as real measured figures — not guesses. The full per-provider numbers are in the appendix at the bottom.

How to read the numbers (plain version)

  • "Match it" = how often a cheaper model gave the same answer our current setup gives. Our own model only repeats itself ~85–90% of the time (AI isn't perfectly consistent), so "matches" means "as close as our current model is to itself".
  • "Cost / month" = projected at 20,000 leads/month and our reply volume. It's the AI model fee only; web-search fees are called out separately for brand-checking.
  • We tested the cheapest model of each provider (that's what these jobs use today): Anthropic Haiku, OpenAI's nano, Google's Flash-Lite. We checked ~50 real items per task (~70 for brand-checking) — enough to be directionally right, not precise to the last point.
